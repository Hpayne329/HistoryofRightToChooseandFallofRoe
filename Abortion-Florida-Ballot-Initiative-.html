The Dobbs Decision

The central tenant of Roe, that women implicitly have a constitutional right to abortion and thus control over their bodies was reaffirmed in the Casey decision, albeit weakened, and subsequently in any number of challenges at the district level, circuit level, and at the Supreme Court. Of the 24 cases on abortion rights seen by the Supreme Court during this period all but 1 upheld the central tenant of Roe, that Women have a constitutional right to an abortion. And these are just cases seen by the Supreme Court, hundreds of cases were heard and rejected at the district and circuit level on the basis of Roe.

Despite almost 50 years of precedent; in 2022 the Supreme Court overruled Roe v. Wade and the Casey decision with the decision in Dobbs v. Jackson Womanâ€™s Health Organization. In this ruling the court held that the constitution does not implicitly confer a right to abortion and that states may choose to regulate it as they see fit, essentially sending the decision back to the states.

This extremely unprecedented move, overruling an established precedent was based on arguments by the majority opinion that access to abortion was not â€œdeeply rooted in this Nationâ€™s history or traditionâ€ nor considered a right when the Due Process clause of the 14th Amendment was ratified in 1868.
